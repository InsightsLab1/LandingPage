ParkRules.com

A park finder & legal research tool that uses AI agents to navigate federal, state, and local regulations and tell you exactly what’s allowed (and what’s not) at any given park.

Key Questions

  • Can I view the sunset here?
  • Are dogs or drones permitted?
  • Is BBQ, campfires, swimming, boating, fishing allowed?
  • Do I need a permit for overnight camping?
  • What food storage or ATV rules apply?

Data Sources & Research

  • Federal Regulations – BLM, National Forests, National Parks rules by district/region
  • State Laws – 50 states × key statutes
  • Municipal Codes – via Municode library
  • County Ordinances
  • Park-Specific Regulations – posted rules, seasonal notices

Architecture

  • GIS data of parks & trailheads, tagged with governing agencies
  • Regulatory text segmented by agency metadata
  • Joined on agency metadata so the AI agent knows which rules apply to each location
  • Interactive map: user clicks a park, AI reads the relevant text and summarizes allowed vs. prohibited activities

Workflow Example

  1. User selects “Bighorn National Forest” on the map
  2. Agent loads applicable USDA Forest Service district regulations
  3. Agent augments with state hunting/fishing statutes
  4. Agent checks local county codes & park postings
  5. Final summary presented: “Backpacking allowed, Fires not allowed without permit, Dogs not allowed, ATV allowed off-trail”

Value Proposition

  • Avoid unintended fines or criminal penalties
  • Quickly find parks that allow your activities
  • Raise awareness of obscure or severe local regulations
  • Leverage AI to digest thousands of pages of legal text in seconds
